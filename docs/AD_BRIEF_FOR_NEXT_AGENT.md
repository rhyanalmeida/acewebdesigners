# Brief: write the next contractor ad

Copy everything below the line into a fresh agent. It is self-contained.

## Your task

Write the script, on-screen text and ad copy for a new Facebook/Instagram video ad for **Ace Web
Designers** (acewebdesigners.com), a small web design agency in Leominster, MA targeting **US
contractors and home-service businesses**.

You have two real ads with real spend behind them: one that worked and one that is failing. Both
transcripts are below, along with every performance number. **Research online as well** — current
2026 direct-response video best practice, contractor/home-services marketing, and Meta creative
benchmarks — but weight the first-party data above anything you read.

Deliver: 3-5 hook variants, one full 30-second script, on-screen text direction, and the ad copy
(primary text, headline, description). Explain each choice against the data.

## THE AD THAT WORKED (2025)

Campaign "New Leads Campaign Video", ad **"video ad 5/29"**, video *"Free Preview Rhyan 1 - 526"*,
**31.6 seconds**. Ran May–Nov 2025 to a general small-business audience at `/landing`.

**Full transcript:**

```
[ 0.0]  Listen up business owners, your website is the first thing that customers see
        and if it's ugly or outdated, they're going to call someone else.

[ 9.0]  That's why I can build you a new website, one that's fast, gets you more
        business, more leads, more customers

[14.2]  and the best part, I can show it to you for free.

[19.3]  No obligation, only pay when you love it.

[22.4]  My name is Ryan and I've been building websites for years now and I'd love to
        get started on your dream.

[28.0]  Click the link down below and we'll talk soon, bye.
```

**On camera:** white collared shirt, plain neutral wall, tight head-and-shoulders, straight to lens,
nothing else in frame, no burned-in captions.

**Ad copy that ran with it:**
- Headline: `Get a Website Preview—Only Pay If You Love It`
- Description: `Zero risk. We design your website first. You decide if it's worth it.`
- Primary text:
  ```
  🚨 Free Website Preview! No upfront cost.
  We'll build you a custom website first—only pay if you love it.
  Perfect for local businesses ready to upgrade their online presence.
  ✅ No risk, no pressure.
  🎥 Watch the video to learn more.
  ```

**Results:** $2,761.60 spend · 33,083 impressions · 1,024 link clicks · **~65 leads** · **several
became paying clients**. Click→lead ≈ **6.3%**.

## Head-to-head — the counter-intuitive part

| | 2025 winner | Current |
|---|---|---|
| CTR | 4.97% | **6.84%** |
| CPM | $82 | **$54** |
| Hold to 25% | **19%** | 30% |
| Click → lead | **6.3%** | 0.69% |
| Quality ranking | — | **BELOW_AVERAGE_35** |

**Read this carefully: the current ad wins on every attention metric and loses 9x on conversion.**
It holds more viewers, costs less per impression, and gets more clicks — and produces almost no
leads. It is buying the wrong kind of attention.

**Do not optimise the new ad for CTR, CPM, or retention.** Optimise for lead quality. Expect the
replacement's attention metrics to look *worse*.

## Why the current ad is failing — diagnosis to build on

1. **It offers a meeting, not a website.** *"Book a free website meeting"* vs the winner's *"I can
   show it to you for free."* Nobody wants a meeting. One no-show's recorded note was
   *"thought i was wix repair"* — he booked without understanding the offer.
2. **No named human, no credential.** The winner says *"My name is Ryan and I've been building
   websites for years now."* The current ad says only "we", and never names anyone.
3. **Features and jargon, not outcomes.** *"fast, modern, mobile-friendly"* then *"transform your
   digital presence"* — a roofer would never use that phrase. The winner promises *"more business,
   more leads, more customers."*
4. **Observation, not consequence.** *"look like they were built in 2009"* states no cost. The winner
   says *"they're going to call someone else"* — a customer phoning a competitor.
5. **The presentation destroys credibility.** Same presenter in both. Collared shirt against a plain
   wall reads as a business owner making an offer; hoodie on a couch with two chihuahuas reads as a
   teenager making a TikTok.
6. **Direct address vs description.** The winner opens *"Listen up business owners"* — a summons. The
   current ad uses "contractor" as an adjective modifying "websites" and doesn't address the viewer
   directly until 13.4s. *(Note: the word "contractor" IS in the current ad's second word, at 0.0s —
   an earlier analysis wrongly claimed it appeared only at 13.4s. The timing claim is withdrawn; only
   the grammatical direct-address point stands.)*

## The audience it is actually reaching

All 11 comments on the ad are from **web developers, not contractors**:

> *"no one wants your AI slop"* · *"clearly made with Claude"* · *"I see the site was built with AI.
> Did y'all do a QA check?"* · *"With that haircut? You build the sites with microsoft paint?"* ·
> *"Us devs are bombarded by these ads every other reel, and they're all identical."*

Those comments carry 11 likes between them — agreement with criticism is what drives the
`BELOW_AVERAGE_35` quality ranking, which in turn inflates CPM. **The new creative must not read as
the saturated "cheap websites" ad genre that developers recognise on sight.**

Placement data reinforces it: Instagram Reels took $236 (36% of spend) and produced **zero** leads.
Audience Network produced 49% of all link clicks and zero leads (now blocked).

## Constraints

- Presenter is **Rhyan** (says "Ryan" on camera). There is also **Valerie**, credited by name in real
  client reviews for social media work.
- Real proof available: 5.0 on Google across 6 reviews; live client sites at dunnconstructionma.com,
  conucotakeout.com, toldhistory.com, hotpotone.net.
- The offer is real: an actual homepage preview is auto-generated before every booked call. This is
  **stronger than what the 2025 winner could claim** — use it.
- The ad set stays optimised on the Lead event, US broad + Advantage+, $20/day. Do not propose
  changing targeting.
- Meta rejects implausible claims and "personal attribute" phrasing. No invented statistics — the
  company has no measured performance figures and previously got in trouble for publishing fabricated
  ones.

## What to research online

- 2026 direct-response video ad structure and hook conventions for local service businesses
- What converts contractors/tradespeople specifically (they are field workers, on phones, skeptical
  of agencies, allergic to jargon)
- Meta 2026 creative guidance: captions, aspect ratio, length, sound-off viewing
- How to avoid the recognisable "cheap website agency ad" genre
- Whether a build-first / preview-first offer has precedent and how it's best framed

## Deliverables

1. **3-5 hook variants** (first 3 seconds), each with a one-line rationale tied to the data.
2. **One complete ~30s script**, timed, in Rhyan's voice — plain spoken English, no jargon. Match the
   winner's ~95 words at ~180wpm.
3. **On-screen text direction** — what appears when. Note that captions are needed (85% watch muted)

   entertainment audiences; recommend a style.
4. **Wardrobe, setting and framing** direction.
5. **Ad copy**: primary text, headline, description — gain-framed and low-pressure like the winner,
   not loss-framed and exclusionary like the current one.
6. **What you'd measure** to know it's working, given that CTR/CPM/retention will likely look worse.

Show your reasoning. Where you disagree with the diagnosis above, say so and explain why.
